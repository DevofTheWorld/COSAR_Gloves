# COSAR: Color Sensing Gloves with Audio Readouts for Visually Impaired Individuals

**A Capstone Research Project** | Limay Senior High School | 2024-2025

---

## 📋 Overview

This repository contains a comprehensive research study on Arduino-based color sensing gloves designed to enhance independence and spatial awareness for visually impaired individuals. The COSAR gloves detect colors in the environment and provide immediate audio feedback, allowing users to interpret color information through sound.

## 🎯 Research Objectives

This study aimed to:

1. **Develop** Arduino-based color sensing gloves with audio readouts
2. **Evaluate** the effectiveness of COSAR in enhancing independence of visually impaired students
3. **Assess** accessibility in terms of:
   - Cost of materials and maintenance
   - Durability of components
   - Affordability and availability
4. **Measure** efficiency through:
   - Accuracy of color detection
   - Clarity of audio feedback
   - User comfort and wearability
5. **Test** specifications including battery life, color range detection, and lighting compatibility

---

## 🔬 Research Design

- **Type:** Quantitative Research (True-Experimental Study Design)
- **Sample Size:** 6 visually impaired students (Grades 11-12, Academic Track)
- **Sampling Method:** Purposive Sampling
- **Participants:** 
  - 3 with Myopia (nearsightedness)
  - 3 with Hyperopia (farsightedness)
- **Data Collection:** Structured questionnaires and observational methods
- **Analysis:** Descriptive statistics, weighted mean, t-tests

---

## 🛠️ Technical Specifications

### Hardware Components
- **Microcontroller:** Arduino Uno
- **Color Sensor:** TCS3200 RGB Color Sensor
- **Audio Output:** DFPlayer Mini MP3 player module + Small Speaker
- **Power:** Small rechargeable battery pack
- **Wearable:** Custom glove with integrated sensors and protective casing

### Key Features
- Real-time color detection using RGB wavelengths
- Audio feedback for color identification
- Wearable design for easy integration
- Low-cost, accessible solution
- Compatible with different lighting conditions (with limitations)

---

## 📊 Key Findings

### Effectiveness Results
- **Color Detection Accuracy:** 66.66% accuracy rate
- **Audio Feedback Clarity:** 100% clear and audible
- **User Comfort:** 66.66% found gloves comfortable and wearable
- **Navigation Efficiency:** 100% successful in facilitating navigation
- **Component Durability:** 66.66% durable components
- **Maintenance Cost:** 66.66% with low maintenance requirements

### Statistical Analysis
- **Mean Effectiveness (Myopia Group):** 2.50
- **Mean Effectiveness (Hyperopia Group):** 2.25
- **T-value:** 2.35336
- **P-value:** 0.03910 (Statistically significant difference at p < 0.05)
- **Result:** Alternative hypothesis accepted - COSAR demonstrates significant accuracy and durability

### Accessibility Assessment
- **Material Cost:** 66.66% of participants found it not highly affordable initially
- **Durability:** 66.66% confirmed durable components
- **Battery Life:** 33.33% confirmed long battery lifespan
- **Color Range Detection:** 66.66% - 100% detection capability
- **Lighting Compatibility:** 33.33% compatible with varying lighting conditions

---

## ✅ Conclusions

The study successfully demonstrated that:

1. **COSAR gloves are practical and user-friendly** - Easy to operate and understand
2. **Reliable color identification** - Consistent color detection with audio readouts
3. **Enhances independence** - Facilitates navigation through school facilities
4. **Affordable and durable** - Cost-effective solution with long-lasting components
5. **Improves spatial awareness** - Allows users to independently identify and navigate based on color cues
6. **Boosts confidence** - Promotes self-reliance in visually impaired users
